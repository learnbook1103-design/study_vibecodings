How to Prepare Carrots

Carrots are versatile root vegetables that can be enjoyed raw or cooked. Proper preparation ensures they are clean, safe, and ready for your culinary creations.

Step 1: Washing

Rinse carrots thoroughly under cold running water. If the carrots are fresh from the garden or have visible dirt, use a vegetable brush to gently scrub the skin. Peeling is optional, as many nutrients are concentrated in or just beneath the skin.

Step 2: Trimming

Cut off the green leafy tops and the thin, fibrous root end. These parts are generally not consumed and can affect the flavor or texture.

Step 3: Peeling (Optional)

If you prefer peeled carrots, use a vegetable peeler to remove the outer skin. Peel away from your body in long, even strokes.

Step 4: Cutting

Carrots can be cut in various ways depending on your recipe:

  • Rounds: Slice crosswise into thin or thick rounds.
  • Sticks (Julienne/Batonnet): Cut into uniform sticks for snacking or stir-fries.
  • Diced: Cut into small cubes for soups, stews, or salads.

Step 5: Storage

Store unpeeled, untrimmed carrots in a cool, dark place or in the crisper drawer of your refrigerator. If peeled or cut, store them in an airtight container with a little water to keep them crisp, changing the water daily. They can last for several weeks when stored properly.
